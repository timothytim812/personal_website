Thermal Analysis

Aim

To develop a MATLAB model to predict the temperature fluctuations over the change in orbital time experienced by the satellite during its operations (i.e. Thermal cycle).

Objectives

  • * Control risk of overheating or cooling
  • * To ensure the components to operate effectively.
  • * Selection of the materials .
  • * Extreme space condtions.

Project Brief

The study started with an comprehensive literature review.

Different methodologies and models were studied and analysed to choose suitable one for the project.

The major things to consider in thermal analysis is all the radiation that is emitted and absorbed by the nano satellite., shown in the below picture

Another important characteristics of the space environment is its high vacuum, which cause heat tansfer from the satellite to space by radiation and the internal heat transferred between the components and satellite by convection and conduction

Due to the heat transfer reasons from satellite to space via components and surface the material properties such as emissivity (ε) and absorptivity (α) were found for different materials. some of them are listed below.

Along with α and ε, the view factor F and albedo, planetary, solar radiation constants and power dissipation value were found.

Then by using all this constants and values in the thermal equilibrium equation (i.e. Thermal input = Thermal output), the equilibrium temperature was calculated.

Thermal cycle is the behaviour of satellite’s temperature over time particularly as the satellite passes between solar exposure period (i.e. during day) and eclipse period in the orbit. Therefore the equation for rate of temperature was derived and thermal constant was found which plays major role in the analysis.

To perform the thermal analysis, transient calculation also known as non-steady calculation by which the change in temperature over change in time for a satellite at equilibrium temperature was found for different aspects.
