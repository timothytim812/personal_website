Droplet Dynamics

ABSTRACT

In this study, the experimental considerations and analytical model for a droplet impinging on a solid surface is performed and built. The model predicts the characteristics of a liquid's impact on Hydrophilic and Hydrophobic Surfaces.

The challenge and complexity of this process requires knowledge of droplet effects such as viscoelastic dissipation, surface tension effects, surface roughness, and wetness all have a role and spreading, as well as the contact line's dynamics. Liquid characteristics, surface wettability, and ambient circumstances all play a role in the fate of a liquid droplet when it collides with a solid surface.

The proposed model gives a good prediction for the maximum spreading ratios of the Newtonian liquid i.e., water for different set of velocities and contact angles on both surfaces. The analytical model that we developed was based on the energy conservation approach. The final relation provides accurate predictions for Weber number, Reynolds number, and Maximum spread factor.

AIM

The project's future results and ideas will help us to predict fuel properties inside the engine and will help to use it efficiently.

Since our team is working on publishing this work, the resources can't be shared for now !
